Teaching Program

Education is key to opening up people's minds for great creativity and innovation. Therefore, this program is an important opportunity for interns with educational background to teach the normal curriculum that entails basic English, science, mathematics, social sciences and religious studies among others that may be identified before placement. The volunteer interns will gain more experience in their desired profession.

Why the program:

The Kenyan Education curriculum, the 8:4:4 system, since1986 has had its fair share of challenges, right from poor education infrastructure , low number of teachers, not forgetting the Teacher student ratio which is 1: more than 50 which is totally a hustle and unacceptable. You will play a crucial role in remedying the shortage of qualified teachers who are unable to handle the overwhelming number of students in a single class. Most of these schools are in remote villages and urban areas.

Who Can Volunteer in the Program?

Access to education, changes life of Kenyan children and it allows them to get exposed and meet opportunities that help them break the vicious cycle of poverty they would otherwise be destined for, as such the volunteers taking up placements in this program must be native English speakers, have a good command in oral and spoken English, be flexible, patient, be passionate about teaching and cross cultural experience.

Apart from being able to read and write, you must be 18years and above, there are no other qualifications other than that.

In primary schools, volunteers will handle students between the ages 10-15 years while in secondary schools it will be between 15 to 18 years depending on the class. Having worked on various projects with "Mzungus", I have seen children get attracted to them and therefore we believe that the volunteers' role will even stretch to that of a true inspiration to the students to perform even better.

Responsibilities

  1. Prepare lesson notes and learning materials
  2. Come up with a teaching strategy/method
  3. Teach classes by way of mini-lectures, discussions and demonstrations to reinforce what has been taught.
  4. Share outputs and strategy with fellow teachers and supervisor
  5. Give assignments to students to gauge their understanding

Duration of Volunteership

The Kenyan Curriculum of education is divided into three terms/period of studies. The first term runs from January to March, pupils/students break for a one month holiday, then resume from May to July, for a second term. Then final or third term runs from September to November. It's entirely upon the volunteer intern to decide which period fits them best. The minimum duration for volunteering is two 2weeks.
